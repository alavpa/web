First try — Portfolio Attempt

Hi, my name is Alex,

This is my first try to make a portfolio, something that I know I should began 10 years ago, but... you know, I was too busy to spend time on something that could give me any long-term benefit.

Senior Android Development

As I told you at the home page I am an Android developer, a "Senior" Android developer, but at this point, I don't even know what's the meaning of "Senior", not years working on this technology, because I've spent a lot of time doing things wrong until I've realized about my mistake, neither how business works, because every time I've changed company I had to learn from my colleagues about the new framework and how it works. The only thing I think that makes you a "Senior" is confidence. Confidence to raise your hand and say: "I think this is wrong and we should change it" or "I've made this bug and we should fix it asap" the last one is the most hard but we as developers should be responsible of our code and be transparent with our team to help us solve the problem. And that for me is to be a "Senior Developer", admit your own mistakes and be brave to aks to your team for help, we are all human beings, all of us have made mistakes, please, raise your hand and you'll become a better developer

All this I have just said, is theoretical, of course it depends of the kind of company you are working on, because not all the companies have the right environment to create proper applications and admit developers mistakes, if you don't feel safe raising your hand, maybe that's not the company where you should work. Run!. Search another job where you feel confortable, I know is hard getting out of the comfort zone, but it worth it.

After recommend all of you to quit of your company I would like to say what I would try to make on this blog. I think is not going to be techy, I think I could give more help to the community explaining my thoughts and experiencies during my career, because about solid, good practices and clean architecture there are a lot of books and a lot of people that is more expert than me, and to be honert, I am still learning about how I should apply it in my daily work.
